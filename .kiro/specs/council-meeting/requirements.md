# The Council Meeting - Strategic Feature Prioritization

## Introduction

The Council is the strategic board of the anime review platform, consisting of diverse stakeholders with unique perspectives, methodologies, and experiences. This document captures their meeting to analyze the current website state and identify the top 3 features that will maximize usability, retention, and growth potential. From these 3 features, the Council will select 1 highest-priority feature to implement, fix, and test immediately.

## Glossary

- **The Council**: The strategic board consisting of 6 members with diverse backgrounds and perspectives
- **MVP**: Minimum Viable Product - the simplest version of a feature that delivers value
- **Retention**: The percentage of users who return to the platform after their first visit
- **Conversion**: The percentage of users who complete a desired action (quiz, email signup, etc.)
- **Viral Coefficient**: The number of new users each existing user brings (>1.0 means exponential growth)
- **User Journey**: The path a user takes through the website from entry to conversion
- **Engagement Metric**: Measurable user interaction (time on site, pages per session, quiz completion rate)
- **The System**: The anime review web application

## Meeting Agenda

### Phase 1: Council Internal Discussion (Closed Door)
The Council meets privately to analyze the current website and debate features among themselves.

**Process:**
1. Each member shares their assessment of the current website
2. Members propose features based on their expertise
3. Council debates and challenges each other's proposals
4. Through discussion, they narrow down to 3 features
5. They prepare a recommendation for us

### Phase 2: Council Presentation to Us
The Council presents their findings and top 3 feature recommendations.

**Deliverables:**
- Summary of current state analysis
- Top 3 features with rationale
- Pros/cons for each feature
- Initial recommendation

### Phase 3: CEO Decision and Directive
Elon Musk (CEO) reviews the Council's recommendation and delivers the final decision.

**Deliverables:**
- Selection of #1 priority feature
- Clear rationale using first principles
- Success criteria and timeline
- Directive for planning phase

### Phase 4: Planning Phase Handoff
We take the CEO's decision and move into formal planning (design and implementation specs).

## Requirements

### Requirement 1: Council Member Perspectives

**User Story:** As a Council member, I want to share my unique perspective on the current website so that we can identify gaps and opportunities from multiple angles

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE meeting document SHALL capture each Council member's assessment of the current website state
2. EACH Council member SHALL provide feedback based on their unique expertise and background
3. THE assessments SHALL identify both strengths and weaknesses of the current platform
4. THE assessments SHALL reference specific pages, features, or user flows
5. THE assessments SHALL be authentic to each member's personality and communication style

### Requirement 2: Feature Proposals

**User Story:** As a Council member, I want to propose features that align with my expertise so that we consider diverse solutions

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. EACH Council member SHALL propose at least 2-3 features based on their perspective
2. THE proposals SHALL include a brief rationale explaining the expected impact
3. THE proposals SHALL consider the current website state and existing features
4. THE proposals SHALL range from quick wins to strategic long-term investments
5. THE proposals SHALL address usability, retention, or growth objectives

### Requirement 3: Structured Debate

**User Story:** As the Council, we want to debate feature proposals rigorously so that we select the most impactful options

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE debate SHALL allow Council members to challenge each other's proposals
2. THE debate SHALL consider multiple dimensions: user impact, technical feasibility, time, ROI
3. THE debate SHALL surface potential risks and trade-offs for each feature
4. THE debate SHALL allow Council members to build on or refine each other's ideas
5. THE debate SHALL remain focused on the goal: maximize usability and retention
6. THE debate SHALL reflect authentic disagreements and different priorities

### Requirement 4: Top 3 Feature Selection

**User Story:** As the Council, we want to narrow down to 3 features so that we can focus our resources effectively

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Council SHALL select exactly 3 features through debate and consensus
2. THE 3 features SHALL represent a balance of quick wins and strategic value
3. THE 3 features SHALL be implementable within reasonable timeframes (1-4 weeks each)
4. THE 3 features SHALL have clear success metrics defined
5. THE 3 features SHALL address different aspects of the user experience
6. THE selection SHALL document why other features were deprioritized

### Requirement 5: CEO Final Decision

**User Story:** As the CEO, I want to select the #1 priority feature so that the team has clear direction

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE CEO SHALL review all 3 finalist features
2. THE CEO SHALL apply first principles thinking to the decision
3. THE CEO SHALL select exactly 1 feature as the highest priority
4. THE CEO SHALL provide clear rationale for the decision
5. THE CEO SHALL set an aggressive but achievable timeline
6. THE decision SHALL include success criteria and next steps

### Requirement 6: Meeting Documentation

**User Story:** As a stakeholder, I want comprehensive meeting notes so that I understand the context and rationale behind decisions

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE meeting document SHALL capture the Council's internal discussion with authentic dialogue
2. THE document SHALL show the debate process with arguments for and against each feature
3. THE document SHALL document how the Council arrived at their top 3 recommendations
4. THE document SHALL include the Council's formal presentation to us
5. THE document SHALL capture the CEO's final decision and first principles rationale
6. THE document SHALL be written as a narrative that reflects each member's personality
7. THE document SHALL clearly separate the three phases: internal discussion, presentation, CEO decision

### Requirement 7: Actionable Output

**User Story:** As a developer, I want clear direction on what to build next so that I can start implementation immediately

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE selected #1 feature SHALL have a clear problem statement
2. THE feature SHALL have defined success metrics
3. THE feature SHALL have an estimated timeline
4. THE feature SHALL have technical requirements outlined
5. THE output SHALL be ready to convert into a design and implementation spec
6. THE decision SHALL include what NOT to build (scope boundaries)

## Success Metrics

- All 7 Council members provide unique perspectives

- Final decision has clear rationale and buy-in

### Feature Selection Quality

- Features have measurable success criteria
- Features align with business objectives (retention, growth, monetization)

- Success criteria are unambiguous
